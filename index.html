FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you want to know about take-home pay, tax calculations, and how PaycheckDecoder works.

What is PaycheckDecoder?
PaycheckDecoder is a free online salary calculator that shows you exactly how much money lands in your bank account after all taxes and deductions. It supports 21 countries, uses official 2025–2026 tax brackets, and requires no signup or registration. All calculations run instantly in your browser.
How do I calculate my take-home pay?
Open the calculator, enter your gross annual salary, choose your country, and click "Calculate My Take-Home Pay". You will instantly see your net pay, a full line-by-line deduction table, and your effective tax rate. You can also switch between annual, monthly, and weekly pay views.
What is the difference between gross salary and net salary?
Gross salary is your total pay before any deductions — the number on your job offer. Net salary (take-home pay) is what remains after income tax, social security contributions, pension contributions, and other mandatory payroll deductions are subtracted. The gap between gross and net can be surprisingly large; PaycheckDecoder helps you understand exactly why.
How is income tax calculated in the United States?
The US uses a progressive federal income tax with seven brackets ranging from 10% to 37% (2025 rates). The standard deduction ($15,000 for single filers, $30,000 for married filing jointly) first reduces your taxable income. On top of federal tax, Social Security is charged at 6.2% (capped at the wage base of $176,100) and Medicare at 1.45% (plus an additional 0.9% on earnings above $200,000). Most states levy an additional state income tax. PaycheckDecoder lets you enter your state rate manually.
How does the UK PAYE income tax system work?
In the UK, income tax is collected through the Pay As You Earn (PAYE) system. Every worker receives a personal allowance of £12,570 tax-free. Income above that is taxed at 20% (basic rate), 40% (higher rate above £50,270), and 45% (additional rate above £125,140). On top of income tax, Class 1 National Insurance (8% on earnings between £12,570 and £50,270, 2% above) is deducted. These thresholds are frozen through 2028.
What social security deductions apply in Germany?
German employees pay social insurance contributions on top of income tax: pension (Rentenversicherung) 9.3%, health insurance (Krankenversicherung) approximately 8.15%, unemployment insurance (Arbeitslosenversicherung) 1.3%, and long-term care insurance (Pflegeversicherung) 1.7%. The combined employee rate is roughly 20.45% of gross wages. High earners may also owe the Solidarity Surcharge (Soli). Married couples benefit from income-splitting (Ehegattensplitting).
How is salary taxed in India for FY 2025-26?
India's New Tax Regime is now the default for salaried employees. The 2025 Union Budget raised the Section 87A tax rebate so that income up to ₹12 lakh is effectively tax-free under the new regime. A standard deduction of ₹75,000 applies. Progressive rates above ₹12 lakh go up to 30%. The old regime (with deductions like 80C) remains available as an opt-in.
Does PaycheckDecoder include state or provincial taxes?
Yes. For the United States you can enter a custom state income tax percentage; the calculator adds it on top of the federal calculation. For Canada, you select your province from a dropdown and PaycheckDecoder applies the correct provincial tax brackets for Ontario, British Columbia, Alberta, Quebec, Saskatchewan, or Manitoba.
Do UAE and Saudi Arabia have personal income tax?
No. Both countries have zero personal income tax. In the UAE, nationals may contribute 5% to GPSSA pension, but expatriates have absolutely no deductions — gross salary equals net take-home pay. In Saudi Arabia, Saudi nationals contribute 10% to GOSI, but expat workers keep 100% of their earnings. This makes the Gulf among the most attractive salary destinations globally.
What is an effective tax rate?
Your effective tax rate is the percentage of your total gross income that goes to all taxes and compulsory deductions combined. It is always lower than your marginal tax rate (the rate on your last dollar of income) because progressive brackets only apply to the income within each band. For example, a US taxpayer earning $100,000 is in the 22% bracket, but their effective rate is approximately 17%. PaycheckDecoder displays your effective rate after every calculation.
How accurate are the tax calculations?
PaycheckDecoder uses official tax brackets and rates sourced from PwC Worldwide Tax Summaries and official government revenue bodies (IRS, HMRC, ATO, CRA, etc.), updated for 2025–2026. Standard deductions, personal allowances, and mandatory social security contributions are all included. Results are accurate estimates for typical salaried employees. Actual deductions can vary based on pension contributions, employer schemes, tax credits, and local regulations. For personalised advice, consult a qualified tax professional.
Can I see my pay broken down monthly or weekly?
Yes. After calculating, you can toggle the display between Annual, Monthly, and Weekly views. All figures — gross, each deduction, and net take-home — scale accordingly. This is especially useful when comparing a monthly salary offer against weekly freelance income.
Is PaycheckDecoder free? Are there any hidden costs?
PaycheckDecoder is completely free. There is no signup, no subscription tier, no paywall, and no hidden fees. All calculations run locally in your browser using JavaScript — your salary data never leaves your device. The tool will remain free forever.
